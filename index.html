About

I was born in San Diego and raised just outside of it in the city of Chula Vista. I stayed at home for my undergraduate and studied ecology at the University of California, San Diego. I graduated in 2023 with my Bachelors of Science in Ecology, Behavior, and Evolution, and helped with research in the lab of Dr. Elsa Cleland, where I worked on research projects on the California poppy,

(Eschscholzia californica)

.

After completing my undergraduate degree, I joined the Integrative Ecology Lab at Temple University in my current position as a PhD student under the advisorship of Dr. Jocelyn Behm. My research studies the Spotted lanternfly,

(Lycorma delicatula)

(SLF), in particular spatial and geographic charactersitcs of their invasion. I largely work with spatial and spectral data collected from USGS-NASA Landsat, and employ large data geographic analysis. I am preapring for my first field season, as well as looking forward to further research, including utilizing machine learning of Landsat spectral signals for remote detection of SLF.
